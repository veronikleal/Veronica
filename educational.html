Educational Information

BELT SYSTEM AND STUDENT PROGRESSION

Isshinryu follows a structured belt system that helps students grow step-by-step. Each belt represents progress in skill, discipline, and understanding.

Belt Orders:

White: Beginning the journey, learning stances, basic punches, blocks, and kicks.
Yellow: Confidence in basic movements, introduction to simple combinations.
Orange: Stronger technique, better balance, understanding of timing.
Green: More advanced combinations, introduction to full kata sequences.
Blue: Developing power, precision, and control.
Brown: Advanced kata, stronger self-defense application, mental discipline.
Brown: Mastery of fundamentals, understanding the philosophy and complete curriculum of Isshinryu.

Promotion is based on Consistent practice Technique improvement Understanding of kata Good attitude and respect in the dojo Dojo Etiquette & Expectations Respect is the foundation of traditional karate. Students learn how to behave with discipline both inside and outside the dojo.

DOJO ETIQUETTE

Includes the following:

-Bowing when entering or leaving the dojo
-Addressing instructors as Sensei or Sempai
-Keeping the gi clean and worn properly
-No shoes on the training floor
-Listening when instructors speak
-Practicing safely with partners -Maintaining focus and self-control

BASICS OF ISSHINRYU KARATE

Emphasizes practical efficient self-defense with:

-Short natural stances
-Vertical punching for wrist protection
-Fast snap kicks for speed and control
-Compact, direct movements that work in real situations
Kihon ( Basics ):
-Blocks (upper, middle, lower)
-Punches (vertical, ridge-hand, backfist)
-Kicks (front snap, side, roundhouse)
-Stances (seisan dachi, sanchin dachi, neko ashi)
Kata (Traditional forms):
-Seisan
-Seiuchin
-Naihanchi
-Wansu
-Chinto
-Kusanku
-Sunsu (Signature Isshinryu's kata)
Kumite (Sparring):
-Timing, distancing, and footwork
-Defensive and offensive techniques
-Respect toward training partners
-Safe use of protective equipment
